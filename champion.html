Subclass Features

Improved Critical Hit (Requires Level 3)

Improved Critical Hit
The number you need to roll a Critical Hit while attacking is reduced by 1. This effect can stack.

 The most significant subclass feature of Champion. By default, a player has 5% crit chance (9.75% with advantage). With this passive, that chance doubles to 10%. When considering advantage on attack rolls, this boosts an attack's crit chance to 19%.
Remarkable Athlete: Jump (Requires Level 7)

Remarkable Athlete: Jump
You're a master of your own body, your athletic prowess extended beyond the usual. Your Jump distance is increased by 3m.

 Synergizes well with the Jump spell. Jumping is one of the best ways to reach your enemy until you acquire the best method in Act 3.
Remarkable Athlete: Proficiency (Requires Level 7)

Remarkable Athlete: Proficiency
You're a master of your own body, your athletic prowess extended beyond the usual. You can add half of your Proficiency Bonus to any Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution Checks that you are not Proficient in.

A decent passive that makes the fighter more proficient in various checks. The checks are as follows: Strength (Athletics) and Dexterity (Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth)
Additional Fighting Style (Requires Level 10)

Additional Fighting Style
Select one additional fighting style: Archery, Defence, Duelling, Great Weapon Fighting, Protection, Two-Weapon Fighting.

 This allows the Champion subclass to have two fighting styles (Level 1 and Level 10). For optimization, the two fighting styles to take are Defence and Great Weapon Fighting.

Level 4 (Feat 1):

Great Weapon Master Great Weapon Master
When attacking with Heavy melee weapons you are Proficient with, Attack Rolls take a -5 penalty, but their damage increases by 10. is the recommended feat for level 4. With the proper party composition, the -5 attack roll can be negated (i.e. advantage, bless). The +10 damage at level 4 is significant, especially for destroying walls/chests. The effect can also be toggled on/off, adding an extra layer to a very simple class.

Level 8 (Feat 3):

Savage Attacker Savage Attacker
When making melee weapon attacks, you roll your damage dice twice and use the highest result. is the recommended feat for level 8. SA does stack with Great Weapon Fighting Great Weapon Fighting
When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack with a Two-Handed melee weapon, that die is rerolled once. in an interesting way. GWF checks if the player rolls either a 1 or 2 damage roll, and rerolls them. SA uses the newly rerolled results as the base for its passive effect, and chooses the largest individual numbers rolled overall (not the largest set of numbers).

Example: Greatsword attack roll (2d6 die). The initial result is 1 + 5. Since a 1 was rolled, GWF rerolls the first die (1) once. Let's say the reroll result is 3. The current result is now 3 + 5. SA takes that and rerolls both numbers. Let's say the SA rolls 2 + 6. Now, SA will take the largest of both numbers to create a set of 3 + 6. This effect is applied to elemental weapon damage as well.

Level 12 (Feat 4):

Alert Alert
You gain a +5 bonus to Initiative and can't be Surprised. is the recommended feat for level 12. The +5 initiative is to assure that the fighter gets to be one of the first people to take their turn. A fighter at level 12 can easily dispatch a few enemies in one turn.
